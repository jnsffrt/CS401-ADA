Rating inflation over time

Our quest to find the secrets behind the ratings begins with an analysis of inflation. Indeed, the data covers the period 2004 - 2017, and it seems that users are rating better and better over time. Figure 1 clearly shows that average scores increase over time.

Time series decomposition

Inflation is due to a general trend, but there are several other time parameters that could impact ratings. We want to correct the general inflation trend, but without taking into account noise or seasonal effects (e.g. presence of beer festivals, conferences, promotions, etc.). To exclude these effects, we decomposed the time series of scores into 3 series using an additive model: trend, seasonal effect and noise. We can see from Figure 2 that these effects are indeed noticeable.

Inflation correction

Now it is time to correct the inflation and the increasing trend of ratings. We computed a reference average at the beginning of the period and we applied a correction to the ratings by dividing them by the ratio of the current average to the reference average. The corrected rating averages are shown in Figure 3.

NLP predictions

Subsection 1

Users can leave text comments in addition to their digital notes. We all have different representations of numerical values, but texts are richer and can help us highlight differences between the comment and the final score given.
To analyze this, we used the pre-trained NLP model nlptown/bert-base-multilingual-uncased-sentiment, which for each comment predicts an our between 1 and 5 corresponding to the text.
